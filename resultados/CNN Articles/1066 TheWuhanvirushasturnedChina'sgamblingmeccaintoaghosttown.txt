Article Title: The Wuhan virus has turned China's gambling mecca into a ghost town, Posted On: 02-03-20, At: https://www.cnn.com/asia/live-news/coronavirus-outbreak-02-03-20-intl-hnk/index.html
An attendant checks the temperature of a tourist at the entrance to the Galaxy Macau casino and hotel in Macau, China, on Friday, January 24. Billy H.C. Kwok/BloombergThere isn't a single face exposed in Macao's cavernous Galaxy casino. Everyone is wearing a mask, including the croupiers, waitresses and security guards -- who happen to vastly outnumber the scattered customers gambling at blackjack and roulette tables.Visitors only momentarily drop their masks at the entrances to the casino, to pose for thermal cameras on the lookout for the deadly Wuhan coronavirus that has killed hundreds of people in mainland China and infected thousands more.The outbreak has left the free-wheeling, semi-autonomous Chinese territory of Macao shell-shocked.Last year the city received almost 40 million visitors. Now, streets and squares once teeming with tourists from mainland China are empty.
