FARMINGTON, CT (WFSB) — A doctor at the University of Connecticut’s hospital has been arrested after intentionally coughing on and hugging nurses.
UConn Police said the actions of Cory Edgar, 48, caused “a substantial amount of alarm” at the Farmington facility’s outpatient surgery clinic on Thursday.
He was issued a misdemeanor breach-of-peace summons.
Officials said the witnesses and medical workers believed Edgar was purposely disregarding space and safety concerns amid the coronavirus pandemic. He has not tested positive for the virus.
Edgar is an assistant professor in the Department of Orthopedic Surgery at UConn Health as well as a physician for the university’s athletic teams.
He was not at work on Monday. UConn Health would not comment to TV station WFSB about his employment status.
